# CycleWarden — unified product thesis

Status: active product definition  
Issue: #9  
Date: 2026-07-23

## Product

CycleWarden is one integrated AI-native product development system around human and AI collaborators.

It combines:

- a usable product workspace;
- a ready application foundation and generator;
- a deterministic evolution kernel;
- modern research and product discovery;
- governed coding-agent execution and sandboxing;
- independent verification and evidence;
- authorized release, deployment and rollback;
- product-outcome measurement;
- controlled learning and meta-improvement;
- interoperability with repositories, tools and other agents.

The Evolution Engine is CycleWarden's process-control core. It is not the only product surface. The workspace, foundation, research, execution, verification, operations and learning modules together form the product.

## The chip-fabrication analogy

A valuable semiconductor process is not one machine. It is a tightly controlled integrated system of design tools, process stages, metrology, defect detection, materials, provenance, yield learning and accumulated know-how.

CycleWarden should be built the same way:

- the workspace and product foundation are the design environment and starting substrate;
- models and agents are interchangeable production tools;
- the deterministic kernel is the process-control system;
- research and product discovery choose the design and experiment;
- sandboxes and execution backends are controlled fabrication environments;
- tests, reviews, metrics and user outcomes are metrology;
- policy gates are process rules;
- provenance and event history are the manufacturing record;
- deployment and rollback are controlled release operations;
- failed cycles are yield-learning data;
- measured skills and meta-improvements are process-node improvements;
- cross-project benchmarks show whether the whole process generalizes.

The moat is the integrated process, evidence corpus, safety rules, evaluation infrastructure and accumulated product-development learning—not one prompt, one agent or one package.

## Product promise

> Define or attach a product once. CycleWarden researches what should happen, builds it through governed agents, verifies and ships it safely, measures the outcome and improves later cycles without surrendering control or inventing unsupported claims.

Given a product objective, repository and explicit boundaries, CycleWarden should:

1. create or attach a usable product workspace and foundation;
2. persist a resumable evolution cycle;
3. inspect the repository, architecture, product context, checks and trust boundaries;
4. research technologies, competitors, repositories, papers, incidents and user signals;
5. produce multiple evidence-backed opportunities and preserve contradiction;
6. select a reversible experiment that reduces important uncertainty;
7. authorize an interchangeable agent or command runner for the exact approved scope;
8. execute inside a capability-negotiated sandbox and isolated branch;
9. run independent technical, security, policy and product evaluators;
10. create reviewable change and provenance artifacts;
11. explicitly authorize release or deployment and preserve rollback;
12. measure technical and product outcomes;
13. recommend keep, iterate, reject or rollback;
14. retain applicable learning only after later measured benefit;
15. expose the full lifecycle through one coherent user experience.

## Unified module boundary

### Product Workspace and Foundation owns

- product brief, roadmap and human workflow;
- project creation/attachment and generated application foundation;
- auth, data, security and integration baseline;
- review, approval, progress, outcome and learning surfaces.

### Evolution Kernel owns

- cycle state, legal transitions, checkpoints, recovery and migration;
- autonomy/risk authorization and exact approval;
- durable typed handoffs between modules;
- terminal verdict and rollback state.

### Research Intelligence owns

- decision framing and retrieval plans;
- source provenance, freshness, confidence and contradiction;
- user/problem discovery;
- opportunity portfolios and experiment decisions.

### Execution and Sandbox owns

- agent and command adapters;
- containment capabilities and resource bounds;
- isolated workspaces, branches and activity records;
- change and rollback artifacts.

### Verification and Operations owns

- independent technical/security/policy checks;
- CI, review, provenance and attestations;
- authorized release, deployment and rollback;
- operational evidence and incidents.

### Measurement and Learning owns

- technical and product outcome evaluation;
- keep/iterate/reject/rollback decisions;
- memory and skill consumption;
- paired evaluation, promotion, expiry and retirement.

Agents may propose or execute through these modules. They do not own state, permissions, truth, completion, release authority or self-improvement claims.

## Proof threshold

A state machine, CLI, starter application or research report alone is not proof of CycleWarden.

The product becomes meaningfully proven when the same integrated flow:

```text
workspace objective
→ understanding and research
→ accepted experiment
→ sandboxed implementation
→ independent verification
→ authorized release
→ measured outcome
→ evaluated learning
```

succeeds on CycleWarden and at least one unrelated real project, while a human can reconstruct the decision, authorization, evidence, contradictions, changes, release and outcome.

## Implementation sequencing

CycleWarden may be built in vertical integration milestones:

1. durable product understanding;
2. research-to-decision;
3. decision-to-change;
4. change-to-outcome;
5. continuous measured evolution.

These milestones are incomplete slices of the same product, not separate product directions. Later modules may be inactive until safety prerequisites exist, but they remain required in the destination architecture.

## Non-goals

- a proprietary foundation model;
- ungoverned automatic merge, deployment or production mutation;
- pretending temporary workspaces are secure sandboxes;
- multi-agent theater without clear module responsibility and measured performance;
- treating technical test success as sufficient product value;
- learning promotion without later comparable evidence;
- claims of sentience or guaranteed monotonic improvement;
- splitting CycleWarden into competing product identities that do not share one lifecycle.
